How to Read the Roast Profile Chart

The roast profile chart visualizes the transformation of the coffee beans during the roasting process. Here's how to interpret the chart:

  • Bean Temperature (BT): The main line showing the actual temperature of the coffee beans over time.
  • Rate of Rise (RoR): Indicates the rate at which the bean temperature is increasing. A declining RoR is typical in a well-controlled roast.
  • Drum Temperature (DT): Represents the temperature inside the roaster drum.
  • Power, Fan, and Drum Speed Settings: Displayed as step lines showing adjustments made during the roast to control the heat application and airflow.
  • Key Phases:
    • Drying Phase: From the start until the beans turn yellow. Moisture is being removed.
    • Maillard Phase: Beans begin to brown due to sugar caramelization and Maillard reactions.
    • First Crack: Beans expand and release steam, marking the start of development phase.

Understanding this chart helps in appreciating the craft that goes into roasting your coffee to perfection.
